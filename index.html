Why Epicodus? Programming?

I moved to Portland last February after relocating back to the Northwest in August. Since moving back, I haven't had a good sense of what I want to do in my career. Having worked mostly service-based jobs in my life, it is very easy for me to fall back into them, but I have never enjoyed service work.

I've been looking for a new opportunity to try something new. Earlier this summer my cousin, who is a front-end web programmer, sent me a link to Epicodus' women's only class. I've considered programming as something I would be interested in learning and developing a career around, but when I've asked relatives about how to get into this field, they've mostly said that I need to either learn on my own or get a BS, then MS, in Computer Science. Both of these options seemed alternately daunting or financially prohibitive.

I've fiddled around with the CSS in my tumblr before and found that I enjoyed looking for patterns and experimenting with the code. However, fiddling with little understanding is frustrating in my experience. I applied to the women's only class, but struggled to complete the tests. After working on the tests for several weeks, I saw that there was a part-time beginners class available and decided that this would be a better fit for me. This experience makes me think that programming might be something that I can become good at and enjoy. I'm excited to learn more about CSS, JavaScript, and beyond!

I struggle with being self-motivated, but am very socially motivated when it comes to learning. Therefore, when I learned about Epicodus I was very interested, particularly because it has the iniative to specifically support women's careers in tech. This seemed to me like a very good environment to learn in for me and it is affordable.
